About Me

Welcome. I’m Malcolm M. Munoko, a licensed Radiographer and Medical Imaging Scientist driven by a commitment to excellence, innovation, and global health impact. My journey in diagnostic radiography spans over five years, covering clinical practice, academic mentorship, humanitarian health, and international collaboration. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Radiography and have developed core expertise in X-Ray, Ultrasound/ Sonography, CT Scan, MRI Scan, and Nuclear Medicine imaging. I’ve worked across diverse healthcare environments; from private hospitals and teaching institutions to field clinics in humanitarian and low-resource or conflict-affected regions settings.

Currently, I serve in multiple capacities:

  • Radiographer and Sonographer at IRC at Kakuma Refugee Camp and LifeCare Hospitals.
  • Lecturer in Medical Imaging Sciences.
  • Common Data Elements(CDE) Reviewer for the ACR–RSNA–ASNR, contributing to global radiology data standardization.
  • Researcher, Trainee and Participant in IAEA Nuclear Medicine programs, including INCAPS4 study and Radiation safety and protection studies.

    • My professional focus lies at the intersection of advancing diagnostic imaging, radiological safety, and nuclear medicine education. Through specialized training with the IAEA, KAERI, KAIST, and regional cardiac imaging programs, I continue to expand my capacity to deliver accurate, safe, and patient-centered imaging services. I am passionate about bridging high-end imaging technology with real-world impact, particularly in low-resource or conflict-affected regions. This has fueled my participation in international workshops, fellowships, and technical missions aimed at enhancing global imaging standards and strengthening health systems.

    Beyond the clinic, I value teamwork and leadership; whether mentoring junior radiographers, contributing to research, or captaining Kenya’s National American Football Team. This platform shares my ongoing work, collaborations, and insights in Radiography, Nuclear Medicine, and Global Health Security.

    Academics

    I hold an undergraduate degree in Radiography(JKUAT), with a Diploma in Medical Coding and additional training in Monitoring and Evaluation(AMREF International University). My academic journey reflects a deep commitment to diagnostic imaging excellence and healthcare systems development. I have further specialized in nuclear medicine through participation in IAEA-led noninvasive cardiology research (INCAPS), and contribute to international standards as a reviewer for the ACR-RSNA Common Data Elements initiative. My academic focus is driven by a passion for improving healthcare outcomes through precision imaging and global collaboration.

    Profession

    My career is a bold intersection of clinical excellence, humanitarian outreach, and global collaboration.
